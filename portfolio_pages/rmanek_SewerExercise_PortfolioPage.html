Boston Curb Flooding

Summary

The goal of this exercises was to understand the design thought process below:

  • Context: The situation in which the problem is occurring.
  • Problem & Solution: The problem is the issue at hand within the context. The solution is the way to overcome said issue.
  • Goals of Project: How will the project be approached in order to achieve the desired solution.

1. Context

Throughout Boston, there are instances seen on the left - where its residents are subject poor sewer systems that easily get clogged up by fallen leaves and debris, with no way of cleaning them. The result is a sidewalk crossing that is flooded, and makes walking along & across the street difficult.

2. Problem & Solution

The problem at hand boils down to the fact that there is no efficient way to clear out the leaves that rest on top of the sewer grating. As a result, every time it rains, they collect atop of the grating - clogging the inlets. Furthermore, because they are not cleaned frequently (due to the time required to sweep everything together and off the grate). The sidewalks stay flooded for days.


A possible solution for this issue could be to create a more efficient way to collect the leaves, thus allowing for more frequent cleaning to help reduce the risk & period of flooding.

3. Design

One possible design that would help achieve this solution can be seen below. Whereby, a tray contained slits/grates is placed with its open end flush with the surface of the road. This would allow the water to pass through the slits, whilst collecting the leaves in a centralised location - below surface level - to decrease the effect of flooding.


Additionally, the tray is removable, allowing for easy cleanup once it gets filled with leaves, reducing the time required - and increasing the frequency of the clean-ups.
